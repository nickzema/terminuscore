Part One: Core System Overview

The EZd6 Rule

Roll 1d6, meet or beat the target number to succeed.

Difficulty Scale

  • Easy (2): Simple tasks, slow walkers
  • Normal (3): Standard actions, normal threats
  • Hard (4): Challenging actions, fast infected
  • Very Hard (5): Extreme danger, elite threats
  • Super Hard (6): Near-impossible, ultimate challenges

Core Mechanics

1's ALWAYS FAIL - Critical failures, devastating consequences

6's ALWAYS SUCCEED - Critical success, best possible outcome

6's EXPLODE - On combat rolls, deal 1 Strike and roll again

Boons & Banes

  • BOON: Roll an extra d6, choose highest result
  • BANE: Roll an extra d6, choose lowest result
  • Multiple boons/banes stack

Resolve System

  • Start each session with 3 Resolve
  • Earn 1 Resolve on any failed roll (limit once per turn)
  • Earn 1 Resolve for excellent roleplay (GM reward)
  • SPEND Resolve: Add to any roll (except 1's, cannot use on Miracle rolls)
  • SPEND 5 Resolve: Replenish your Plot Armor

Plot Armor

  • Receive 1 Plot Armor at session start
  • Spend to re-roll any single die
  • Can be replenished with 5 Resolve

Time & Distance

DISTANCE:

  • Near: Less than 30 feet
  • Far: More than 30 feet

TIME PER TURN:

  • Move Action: 30 feet of movement
  • Turn Action: Attack, Use item, Interact, Move again
  • Free Action: Draw weapon, Shout, Drop item

INITIATIVE:

Unless ambushed, survivors always go first, in any order they choose.

Part Two: Character Creation

Step 1: Choose Your Survivor Path

THE FIGHTER

Former police, military, or just someone who learned to survive

Stats: 3 Strikes, Armor 5, Miracle 6

Boons: Melee Combat, Strength

Special Abilities:

  • Last Stand: Free action - Sacrifice 1 Strike to make an extra attack this turn
  • Tactical Awareness: Gain a boon when defending against human attackers
  • Heavy Weapons: When using two-handed weapons (sledgehammer, axe), crit on 5-6

THE SCAVENGER

Quick, clever, and always finds what the group needs

Stats: 3 Strikes, Armor 6, Miracle 6

Boons: Stealth, Scavenging

Special Abilities:

  • Nimble: Free action - Cancel one melee Strike against you
  • Lucky Find: When scavenging, gain a boon to rolls
  • Sneak Attack: When attacking surprised or flanked enemies, attack twice

THE MEDIC

Doctor, nurse, EMT, or battlefield medic

Stats: 3 Strikes, Armor 5, Miracle 5

Boons: Medicine, Resist Disease

Special Abilities:

  • Field Medicine: Free action - Heal 1 Strike on ally by taking 1 Strike yourself
  • Emergency Care: 1/day (regain for 5 Resolve) - Roll 1d6 per target in Near range, heal 1 Strike on 4+
  • Immunity Boost: Party gains boon against infection/disease

THE SURVIVOR

Tough as nails, been through hell and back

Stats: 5 Strikes, Armor 6, Miracle 6

Boons: Endurance, Strength

Special Abilities:

  • Iron Will: Boon against fear and psychological effects
  • Shrug It Off: Sacrifice 1 Strike to deal an extra Strike in melee
  • Unstoppable: When reduced to 0 Strikes, roll 4+ to remain conscious with 1 Strike

THE LEADER

Sheriff, teacher, community organizer - someone people follow

Stats: 3 Strikes, Armor 5, Miracle 5

Boons: Leadership, Persuasion

Special Abilities:

  • Inspiring Presence: Can spend your Resolve on any ally you can see
  • Rally: Once per scene, all Near allies gain a boon on their next roll
  • Tactical Command: During group actions, grant boon to coordinated tasks

THE ENGINEER

Mechanic, electrician, craftsperson - keeps things running

Stats: 3 Strikes, Armor 5, Miracle 6

Boons: Repair, Crafting

Special Abilities:

  • Jury-Rig: Can repair or create items with scavenged parts
  • Fortify: Improve defenses of safe houses (takes time)
  • Improvise: Create makeshift weapons, tools, or traps with boon

THE HUNTER

Outdoorsperson, sniper, tracker

Stats: 3 Strikes, Armor 5, Miracle 6

Boons: Ranged Combat, Tracking

Special Abilities:

  • Dead Eye: Boon on ranged attacks
  • Tracker: Can follow trails and identify signs
  • Silent Kill: Stealth kills against unaware targets require no roll

THE RUNNER

Parkour enthusiast, athlete, courier

Stats: 3 Strikes, Armor 6, Miracle 6

Boons: Acrobatics, Athletics

Special Abilities:

  • Fast Movement: Can move twice in one turn without using Turn Action
  • Escape Artist: Boon on Armor and Miracle saves
  • Evasive: Only be knocked prone by a critical hit

Part Three: Combat & Survival Rules

Combat Basics

ATTACKING:

  • Roll 1d6, need 3+ to hit
  • Apply boons/banes as appropriate
  • On hit, target takes 1 Strike
  • On 6, deal 1 Strike and roll again (exploding)

TO-HIT MODIFIERS:

  • Near range, clear shot: Normal (3)
  • Far range: Hard (4)
  • Target in cover: Bane to attack
  • Flanking/surprise: Boon to attack
  • Shooting in melee: Bane to attack

BEING HIT:

When hit, you may:

  1. Roll Armor Save: Roll 1d6, meet or beat your Armor number to ignore the Strike
  2. Take the Strike: Reduce your Strike total by 1

STRIKES (Health):

  • Most survivors have 3-5 Strikes
  • At 0 Strikes: Unconscious or dead (GM discretion)
  • Tough survivors might get one last 4+ roll to stay conscious at 0

Weapon Types

MELEE WEAPONS:

  • Light (knife, hatchet): No modifier
  • Medium (bat, crowbar, machete): Standard damage
  • Heavy (axe, sledgehammer): Two-handed, crit on 5-6, bane if exhausted
  • Improvised (pipe, chair leg): Bane to attack, breaks on 1

RANGED WEAPONS:

  • Pistol: Near range normal, Far range hard, common ammo
  • Rifle: Far range normal, powerful but loud
  • Shotgun: Near range with boon, useless at Far range, devastating
  • Bow/Crossbow: Silent, reusable ammo, needs time to reload
  • Thrown (molotov, grenade): One use, affects area

AMMUNITION:

Track ammo loosely. After extended combat, roll 1d6:

  • 1-2: Low on ammo (disadvantage)
  • 3-4: Half depleted
  • 5-6: Plenty left

Special Combat Actions

HEADSHOT (Walkers):
• Declare headshot before rolling
• Need 5+ to hit (Very Hard)
• Instantly kills standard walker
• Use Resolve to improve chances
STEALTH KILL:
• Against unaware target
• Roll Stealth vs. their Awareness (usually 3)
• Success = silent kill, no alert
• Failure = alert nearby threats
GRAPPLE/ESCAPE:
• Opposed roll: highest wins
• While grappled: bane on all actions except breaking free
• Walkers grapple to bite (see Infection)
DESPERATE DEFENSE:
• When surrounded or overwhelmed
• Declare you're fighting defensively
• Bane to attacks, but boon to Armor saves
COVERING FIRE:
• Use Turn Action to suppress area
• Allies gain boon to cross/escape
• Consumes extra ammo

Infection & Death

WALKER BITES:

When a walker successfully grapples and bites:

  • Immediately take 1 Strike
  • Must make Miraculous Save (roll your Miracle number)
  • Success: No infection
  • Failure: Infected - will turn in 1d6 hours (GM tracks secretly)

INFECTED STATUS:

  • No cure
  • Character becomes increasingly ill
  • Final moments are between player and GM
  • Can choose heroic sacrifice
  • Will reanimate as walker if brain isn't destroyed

DEATH:

  • At 0 Strikes: Usually unconscious or dying
  • GM may allow "death saves" (Miracle roll)
  • Heroic sacrifices always allowed
  • Always destroy the brain of the dead

Environmental Hazards

EXHAUSTION:

  • After extended action without rest: bane to all rolls
  • Need 6+ hours sleep to recover
  • Can push through with Resolve

STARVATION:

  • After 3 days without food: lose 1 Strike per day
  • These Strikes only recover with proper meals

DEHYDRATION:

  • After 1 day without water: bane to all rolls
  • After 2 days: lose 1 Strike per day
  • Critical - faster than starvation

EXPOSURE:

  • Extreme weather without shelter: Miracle save or lose 1 Strike
  • Hypothermia/heatstroke serious threats

NOISE:

  • Gunshots: Alert walkers within Far range
  • Loud actions: Alert walkers within Near range
  • Stealth: Move slowly and quietly to avoid detection

Part Four: Resources & Scavenging

Scavenging System

When searching for supplies, roll 1d6 + BOONS:

Roll Result
1 Nothing usable, walkers alerted
2 Nothing found
3-4 Common supplies (food, water, basic items)
5 Useful find (weapon, medicine, tools)
6 Jackpot! (multiple items or rare find)

LOCATION MODIFIERS:

  • Pharmacy: Boon to find medicine
  • Gun Store: Boon to find weapons/ammo (likely looted/dangerous)
  • Grocery Store: Boon to find food (mostly empty by now)
  • Hardware Store: Boon to find tools/supplies
  • Residential: General supplies
  • Abandoned Camp: Potentially dangerous, possibly well-supplied

TIME & RISK:

  • Quick search: 10 minutes, bane to roll
  • Thorough search: 1 hour, normal roll
  • Deep search: 3+ hours, boon to roll, higher danger

Supply Management

FOOD RATIONS:

  • Need 1 meal/day to avoid starvation
  • Canned goods (never spoils)
  • Dry goods (flour, rice - needs cooking)
  • Fresh food (spoils in days)
  • Hunting/foraging (requires skill/time)

WATER:

  • Need 1 bottle/day to avoid dehydration
  • Bottled water (safe)
  • Found water (needs purification)
  • Rain collection (needs container)
  • Stream/lake water (risky)

MEDICINE & SUPPLIES:

  • First Aid Kit: Heal 1 Strike (limited uses)
  • Antibiotics: Prevent infection from injuries
  • Painkillers: Ignore 1 Strike of penalty for 1 scene
  • Bandages: Stabilize bleeding wounds

AMMUNITION:

Track by category, not individual bullets:

  • Plentiful: No issues
  • Limited: Bane when using
  • Nearly Empty: 4+ to not run out
  • Empty: Must scavenge more

FUEL:

Essential for vehicles and generators:

  • Cars: Typically 1-2 tanks found
  • Generators: Loud, attract attention
  • Cooking: Propane, firewood, etc.

Crafting & Modification

With proper skills and time, create:

IMPROVISED WEAPONS:

  • Nail bat (bat + nails): Better than standard bat
  • Spear (pole + knife): Reach weapon
  • Molotov cocktail (bottle + fuel + rag): Area damage
  • Suppressor (oil filter + duct tape): Reduce gun noise once

DEFENSIVE ITEMS:

  • Makeshift armor (+1 to Armor save, heavy)
  • Barricades (secure locations)
  • Tripwires (alert system)
  • Spike traps (1d6 Strike to walkers)

UTILITY ITEMS:

  • Water filter (purify water)
  • Radio repair (long-range communication)
  • Vehicle repair (get running)

Part Five: Threats & Enemies

Walkers (Zombies)

STANDARD WALKER
The shuffling dead

Strikes: 1 (destroy head to kill)
To-Hit: 3
Speed: Slow, can be outrun
Grapple: On hit, opposed roll to escape or be bitten
Swarm: In groups, overwhelm by numbers
Senses: Attracted to noise, movement, smell
Threat: Low individually, deadly in numbers

WALKER VARIATIONS:

FRESH WALKER (Recently turned)
To-Hit: 3 | Strikes: 2
Special: Slightly faster, not fully decayed
Danger: May still have some coordination
CRAWLER (Partial body)
To-Hit: 4 (harder to spot) | Strikes: 1
Special: Attacks from ground, ambush predator
Danger: Easy to miss until too late
HORDE (Not a single enemy but a mass)
Size: 10-100+ walkers
Mechanics: Cannot be fought, must be avoided
Effects: Overwhelm any position within hours
Escape: Require distraction, stealth, or vehicle
Sound: Create deafening moaning that attracts more

Human Threats

THE DESPERATE
People driven to extremes by survival

Strikes: 1-2 | To-Hit: 3-4
Equipment: Basic weapons, little ammo
Behavior: May beg, threaten, or ambush
Danger: Unpredictable, may be reasonable
BANDITS/RAIDERS
Organized groups that take what they want

Strikes: 2-3 | To-Hit: 3
Equipment: Good weapons, some armor
Tactics: Ambush, intimidation, overwhelming force
Danger: High - experienced and ruthless
BANDIT LEADER
Strikes: 4 | To-Hit: 2 (skilled)
Equipment: Best weapons, armor (save on 4+)
Special: Tactical awareness, rallies others
Danger: Very high - avoid direct conflict
HOSTILE COMMUNITY
Fortified group protecting their territory

Size: 10-50 members
Equipment: Various, well-supplied
Behavior: Defend borders, may negotiate or attack
Danger: Variable - diplomacy possible but risky
THE GOVERNOR/TYRANT
Charismatic leader of hostile group

Strikes: 5 | To-Hit: 2
Equipment: Excellent weapons and armor (save on 3+)
Special: Commands loyalty, tactical genius, ruthless
Minions: Many followers willing to die for them
Danger: Extreme - major antagonist threat

Animal Threats

FERAL DOGS (Pack)
Strikes: 1-2 each | To-Hit: 3
Pack Tactics: If 2+ attacking same target, crit on 5+
Speed: Fast, can chase down runners
Behavior: Desperate and hungry
WILD ANIMALS
Bears, wolves, big cats - now without fear of humans

Strikes: 3-5 | To-Hit: 2-3
Danger: High - avoid when possible

Environmental Threats

COLLAPSING STRUCTURES:

  • Miracle Save (usually 4+) or take 1-3 Strikes
  • Common in decayed cities

FIRES:

  • Spread quickly in ruined buildings
  • 1 Strike per turn in flames
  • Smoke inhalation: Miracle save or pass out

DISEASE:

  • Contaminated water: Miracle save or fall ill
  • Infected wounds: Need antibiotics
  • Illness: Lose 1 Strike, bane to rolls until treated

Part Six: Running the Game

GM Principles (The Referee's Guide)

JUDGMENTS OVER RULES:

The 2-6 difficulty scale is all you need. If players want to attempt something not covered:

  • Decide on a difficulty
  • Consider boons/banes based on situation
  • For truly exceptional attempts, require confirmed crit (roll 6, then roll again to confirm)

MAKE IT MATTER:

  • Every roll should have stakes
  • Success AND failure should move story forward
  • Don't roll if outcome doesn't matter

BE A FAN OF THE SURVIVORS:

  • Want to see them succeed BUT...
  • Put them in impossible situations
  • Force hard choices
  • Make them earn victories

TELEGRAPHING DANGER:

  • Always give warning before major threats
  • "You hear moaning in the distance..."
  • "The floor creaks ominously..."
  • Let them make informed choices about risk

Building Tension

RESOURCE SCARCITY:

  • Start generous, gradually tighten
  • Force choices: "We have 3 bullets and 5 targets..."
  • Medicine running out during illness
  • Vehicle fuel low during escape

TIME PRESSURE:

  • "The horde arrives in 1 hour..."
  • "He's turning and you don't know when..."
  • "The sun sets in 30 minutes..."

MORAL DILEMMAS:

  • Save the child or the supplies?
  • Accept refugees who might be dangerous?
  • Kill someone who's bitten but not turned?
  • Steal from another group to save yours?

RELATIONSHIP DRAMA:

  • Conflicting goals among survivors
  • Romance in the apocalypse
  • Leadership disputes
  • Blame and guilt

Session Structure

  1. OPENING: Recap previous session, award 3 Resolve and 1 Plot Armor, set the scene
  2. EXPLORATION PHASE: Survivors plan and act, scavenging, travel, interaction, build tension gradually
  3. COMPLICATION: Introduce the problem/threat, escalate stakes, force decisions
  4. CLIMAX: Action sequence or major decision, high stakes, high tension, potentially deadly
  5. RESOLUTION: Consequences of actions, moment to breathe, setup for next session
  6. ENDING: Cliffhanger or quiet moment, award any earned permanent benefits, ask "What's your character thinking/feeling?"

Rewarding Survivors

No traditional leveling. Instead, reward through:

STORY MILESTONES:

After major accomplishments, survivors grow:

  • New skill/inclination
  • Additional permanent Plot Armor (start sessions with 2-3)
  • Improved ability
  • Special equipment

FOUND EQUIPMENT:

  • Better weapons (silenced pistol, crossbow)
  • Protective gear (motorcycle armor, riot shield)
  • Useful items (night vision, radio)

SAFE PLACES:

  • Establish and improve settlements
  • Build defenses
  • Create sustainable food/water
  • Attract other survivors

RELATIONSHIPS:

  • Bonds with NPCs matter
  • Community provides resources
  • Personal connections drive stories

Part Seven: Campaign Framework

Campaign Phases

PHASE 1: THE OUTBREAK (Sessions 1-3)

Theme: Chaos, separation, survival

  • Characters meet during initial chaos
  • Basic survival skills developed
  • Learn the rules of new world
  • Lose everything from old life

Key Scenes: The first walker encounter, losing someone important, finding temporary shelter, meeting other survivors

PHASE 2: THE ROAD (Sessions 4-8)

Theme: Journey, hope, betrayal

  • Search for safety/supplies
  • Encounter other groups
  • Face human threats
  • Build group bonds

Key Scenes: First major scavenging run, encountering hostile survivors, finding signs of safe haven, difficult moral choice

PHASE 3: THE COMMUNITY (Sessions 9-15)

Theme: Home, politics, security

  • Establish or join settlement
  • Deal with group dynamics
  • Balance safety vs. freedom
  • Manage resources

Key Scenes: First night in safety, leadership conflicts, defending home from horde, recruiting/exiling members

PHASE 4: THE WAR (Sessions 16+)

Theme: Conflict, philosophy, cost

  • Face major antagonist group
  • Question what you've become
  • Fight for survival of community
  • Determine future of your world

Key Scenes: Negotiation breakdown, major battle/siege, assassination/infiltration, final confrontation, aftermath and cost

Long-Term Campaign Ideas

  • THE CARAVAN: Survivors constantly move, never settling, searching for mythical safe zone
  • THE FORTRESS: Group secures large compound, deals with expansion, politics, raiders, and hordes
  • THE MISSION: Specific goal drives group - finding cure, rescuing loved ones, reaching coast
  • THE DECAY: Watch civilization's remnants crumble, become more savage, lose humanity
  • THE REBUILD: Hope-focused campaign, slowly rebuilding community and society

Ending the Campaign

Know when your story ends:

TRAGIC ENDING:

  • Everyone dies but meaningful
  • One survivor carries on their legacy
  • Sacrifice saves others

BITTERSWEET ENDING:

  • Safety achieved but at great cost
  • Home established, but changed
  • Survival continues but scarred

HOPEFUL ENDING:

  • Community thriving
  • Future for next generation
  • Found purpose beyond survival

AMBIGUOUS ENDING:

  • Victory uncertain
  • New threat emerging
  • Journey continues

Sample Scenario: "The Pharmacy Run"

Setup

The group's safe house has a wounded member. Infection is setting in. They need antibiotics or he dies within 24 hours. The nearest pharmacy is 2 miles away through walker-infested streets.

Scene 1: The Decision

  • Who goes? (Risk spreaders vs. keeping fighters at home)
  • What route? (Fast but dangerous vs. slow but safer)
  • What time? (Daylight visible vs. nighttime dangerous)

Scene 2: The Journey

  • Encounter 1d6 walkers
  • Environmental hazard (collapsed road, fire, etc.)
  • Time pressure building

Scene 3: The Pharmacy

  • Already looted, but medicine might remain
  • Scavenging roll with time limit
  • 1d6 walkers attracted by noise
  • Optional: Other survivors also scavenging

Scene 4: Complication

Roll 1d6:

  1. Horde approaching, must flee
  2. Bandits demand "toll" to leave
  3. Pharmacy is raided by larger group
  4. Another survivor is bitten, begs for help
  5. Find survivor who claims to be doctor
  6. Walkers everywhere, must fight through

Scene 5: The Return

  • Race against time
  • Wounded survivor's condition worsening
  • Medicine works OR not enough/wrong type
  • Consequences of decisions

Outcomes

  • Success: Survivor lives, group bonds
  • Partial: Survivor lives but cost paid
  • Failure: Survivor dies, group must cope
  • Disaster: Multiple casualties, group fractures

Final Thoughts

This Walking Dead adaptation of EZd6 focuses on:

  • SURVIVAL HORROR over heroic fantasy
  • RESOURCE MANAGEMENT creates tension
  • MORAL CHOICES define characters
  • HUMAN THREATS often worse than walkers
  • DEATH IS POSSIBLE (but meaningful)
  • HOPE VS. DESPAIR constant balance

REMEMBER:

  • Keep it fast and brutal
  • Every session should feel dangerous
  • Make supplies matter
  • Force impossible choices
  • Reward creative solutions
  • End on cliffhangers or quiet moments
  • Character death should mean something

The dead walk. The living fight. Your story begins now.
